# Managing a £5M UK Project – PMO Case Study

## 📖 Overview
This case study demonstrates how I would approach the governance, planning, reporting, and risk management of a £5M project in a UK organisation. It reflects my understanding of PMO structure, stakeholder coordination, and delivery control.

---

## 🎯 Project Objective
To deliver a business-critical transformation project on time, within budget, and with strong governance, while maintaining clear stakeholder communication and risk visibility.

---

## 🏗 Project Scope
- Budget: £5M  
- Duration: 12 months  
- Teams involved: Operations, Technology, Finance, Compliance, and External Vendors  
- Delivery focus: Process improvement, reporting enhancement, and operational efficiency  

---

## 🧭 PMO Approach

### 1. Project Initiation
- Define project charter and objectives  
- Confirm scope, budget, timeline, and governance model  
- Identify sponsors, stakeholders, and delivery teams  

### 2. Planning & Governance
- Build project plan with milestones and dependencies  
- Establish RAID log, KPI dashboard, and weekly reporting cadence  
- Set up steering committee and stakeholder review meetings  

### 3. Stakeholder Management
- Map internal and external stakeholders  
- Maintain regular communication through status reports and progress updates  
- Escalate risks and issues early for timely decision-making  

### 4. Risk & Issue Control
- Track risks, assumptions, issues, and dependencies through a structured RAID log  
- Apply mitigation plans for high-priority risks  
- Monitor budget variance and timeline slippage regularly  

### 5. Performance Monitoring
- Track KPIs such as milestone completion, budget control, reporting accuracy, and delivery progress  
- Use dashboard reporting to provide visibility to senior stakeholders  
- Drive corrective actions where performance deviates from plan  

---

## 📊 Key PMO Deliverables
- Project Charter  
- Milestone Plan  
- RAID Log  
- KPI Dashboard  
- Weekly Status Report  
- Steering Committee Update  

---

## ⚠️ Potential Risks
- Delay in approvals from key stakeholders  
- Resource constraints across multiple teams  
- Vendor delivery dependency  
- Budget pressure due to scope change  

---

## ✅ Expected Outcome
With structured governance, proactive risk management, and clear reporting, the project would be better positioned for on-time delivery, budget discipline, and stronger stakeholder confidence.
